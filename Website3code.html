About me

Hi there, my name is Sam Finley. I am finishing my third year of a Bachelor of Science in Kinesiology with a major in Exercise and Health Physiology at the University of Calgary. I have been fortunate to live in many different cultures throughout my life; I was born in Doha, Qatar, and resided in Al Khor, where we stayed for five years before moving to Brookfield, Australia, a small rural community about a 30-minute drive from downtown Brisbane, Queensland. We lived in Brookfield for another five years before moving to Water Valley, Alberta, another small rural farm town an hour's drive from Calgary, Alberta.

My journey into the world of sports and sciences began at Cremona School, where I completed my grade 5 through 12 schooling. It was here that my passion for these fields was ignited. This passion led me to the University of Calgary, where I was accepted to study Kinesiology, allowing me to further explore the field of Sports Science at the Top sports science school in North America!

My commitment to learning led me to volunteer at a rural Physiotherapy clinic during my first summer. I shadowed the head Physiotherapist here, gaining invaluable experience in injury evaluation, treatments, and exercise prescriptions for numerous patient demographics. Last summer, I continued to expand my knowledge by shadowing Chiropractor Dr Greg Uchachz at his clinic in downtown Calgary. This fantastic experience allowed me to witness the intricacies of the Chiropractic world and interact with numerous Olympic, National team, and NHL players. I combined this volunteer experience with my growing love of Concussions as SHRed Concussions hired me as a Research assistant. I will also be helping master's student Kenzie Friesen in her video analysis project on the effects of mouthguards on head acceleration in high school rugby athletes! This is a link to my resume for you to read as well! and I hope you enjoy my latest computer programming projects!

Python

Here are some graphs that I have previously created through Kaggle using simple Python codes. The first graph is comparing 3 different y-axis variables: VO2 (L/min), VE (breaths/min) and VCO2 (L/min), stacked on top of one another and all variables compared to the same x-axis, Time (mins). The second graph is orientated in a similar format with 3 unique variables on the y-axis: FEO2 (BPM), FECO2 (%) and VCO2 (%), against compared to a singular x-axis, VO2 (L/min). Graph 2 also is divided into thirds highlighting the 2 exercise thresholds, Lactate Threshold and Maximal Lactate Steady State Threshold respectively.
